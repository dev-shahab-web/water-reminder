# Monetization

## Monetization Position

Water Reminder should launch with a trustworthy free experience. Monetization should enhance personalization and convenience without blocking the core hydration habit.

## Free Tier

The free tier should include:

- Daily goal tracking.
- Quick-add logging.
- Custom amount logging.
- Basic reminder schedule.
- Recent history.
- Local data storage.

## Potential Premium Features

- Advanced reminder schedules by day of week.
- Smart reminders based on progress pace.
- Custom themes.
- Widgets.
- Extended history and insights.
- Data export.
- Health integration.
- Cloud backup and multi-device sync.

## Recommended MVP Monetization

Do not monetize in the first product build unless required for Play Store validation. Focus on retention, reminder opt-in, and habit-loop quality.

When monetization is introduced, prefer a one-time unlock or low-cost subscription only if ongoing sync or server costs exist.

## Pricing Hypothesis

- One-time premium unlock: suitable for local-only advanced features.
- Subscription: suitable only if cloud sync, backup, or recurring content creates ongoing costs.
- Ads: not recommended because they reduce trust in a wellness utility and can interrupt the logging loop.

## Assumptions

- Initial product value must be proven before pricing.
- Users are sensitive to trust and privacy in personal wellness apps.
- The app can be useful without a backend, which lowers operating cost.
- Monetization should not require architecture changes in MVP.

## Edge Cases

- Premium reminder features may make the free tier feel intentionally weak.
- Subscription without cloud value may feel unjustified.
- Lifetime unlock may underfund future sync infrastructure.
- Regional pricing and taxes require store-level planning.
- Refund and restore purchase flows add product and QA complexity.

## Suggested Improvements

- Add a monetization experiment plan after MVP retention metrics are available.
- Keep premium boundaries around convenience, not essential habit function.
- Add purchase restore requirements before implementing paid features.
- Validate willingness to pay with store listing tests or small user interviews.

## Trade-Offs

- Launching free improves adoption, but delays revenue learning.
- A one-time unlock is user-friendly, but may not support long-term backend costs.
- A subscription can fund ongoing services, but increases user expectations.
- Ads may generate revenue earlier, but conflict with the calm product experience.
